# 14 · Show & Tell · Closing

**Day 2 · 4:30 – 5:00 PM · 30 min** · Hosts: **Jason Lee** and **Matt Pugh**

> Everyone shows. Everyone's proud. Then we name where this goes next.

---

## Session Goal

Every attendee has **publicly demonstrated something they built** and leaves with a concrete next step for the week after the workshop.

## Outline

1. **Rapid-fire demos** — 18 min
   - 90 seconds each, ~12 attendees (scale up/down to fit)
   - Format: what it is, what it does, one thing you learned
2. **Host reflections** — 4 min
   - What surprised us across the room
   - Patterns we saw in what worked and what didn't
3. **Where to go next** — 6 min (see below)
4. **Thanks & closing** — 2 min
   - Partners, facilitators, dinner

## Where to Go Next (host-delivered takeaway)

- **Keep the stack you learned.** Claude Code + GitHub + Vercel + (Neon/Auth0/Resend) is a production-grade toolkit. Don't shop around.
- **Pick 2 commands to master this month.** From the [8-command table](../../resources/Claude/commands/README.md), suggest starting with `/project:init-claude-md` and `/project:review`.
- **Keep iterating your `CLAUDE.md`.** The one you wrote today is v1. Revisit monthly.
- **Join the CITN community** — share what you ship, see what others build.
- **Subscribe to release notes** — Anthropic ships fast. Staying current is cheap.
- **Context7 MCP** — next step for teams who need always-current library docs inside Claude (see [prereqs Bonus Setup](../../_requirements/README.md)).

## Talking Points

- Celebrate **shipped**, not impressive. Someone whose app does one small thing on a real URL has done exactly what this workshop was for.
- Name specific moments from the two days — "when the room clapped for [name] at 3:50," "the moment [name] realized the plan was wrong and rewrote it."
- The partners paid for the food, the venue, and the dinner tonight. Name them by name (see [resources/images](../../resources/images/)).

## Hands-On / Exercises

### Demo format card (for each presenter)

- **What it is** (one sentence) — e.g. "Volunteer signup for our youth team."
- **Live demo** — 60 seconds, one happy-path flow, a real URL
- **What I learned** — one sentence — e.g. "I over-scoped and cut three features in Build Block 1, and I'm glad I did."

## Notes for the Hosts (Jason & Matt)

- 90 seconds per demo is *not enough* and also *perfect*. Keep it moving. Use a visible timer.
- If 12 people demo and you have time left, open the floor for one or two longer deep-dives.
- Keep the closing charge **actionable, not inspirational**. Tell them what to do Monday, not how to feel on Sunday.
- Dinner is at 6:30 — announce the logistics (where, how to get there) before you leave the stage.
