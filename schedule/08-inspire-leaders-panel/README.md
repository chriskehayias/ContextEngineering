# 08 · Inspire Leaders to Innovation — Panel

**Day 1 · 4:20 – 5:00 PM · 40 min** · Hosts: **Jason Lee** and **Matt Pugh** · Panelists: _TBD_

> The day ends with leaders talking to leaders — what actually moves the needle back home.

---

## Session Goal

Leaders in the room leave with **one concrete thing they'll do Monday** and **one thing they'll stop doing** regarding AI in their church or organization.

## Outline

1. **Panelist intros** — 5 min (45 sec each)
2. **Moderated questions** — 20 min
3. **Audience Q&A** — 12 min
4. **Closing charge** — 3 min
   - Tee-up Day 2 and dinner

## Suggested Panel Questions

Pick 4–5 of the following — don't try to cover all of them.

- **Where did you start?** What was the first AI tool or workflow you actually adopted, and why that one?
- **What failed?** Name an AI initiative that didn't land and what you learned.
- **Change management.** How do you get a volunteer-heavy team on board with new tooling?
- **The "we already tried that" problem.** How do you distinguish a real blocker from AI skepticism?
- **Budget & buy-in.** How do you justify a Claude subscription or API spend to a board?
- **Where does AI *not* belong** in ministry tech? Where have you drawn a line?
- **Monday morning.** If someone goes home from this workshop and can do exactly one thing, what should it be?

## Talking Points for the Hosts

- Keep panelists specific. If you hear "we should really think about AI governance," push for "name a decision you made this month."
- Protect the audience Q&A time — it's what makes this different from a keynote.
- The closing charge should land with the energy of "tomorrow you'll build the thing you've been circling."
